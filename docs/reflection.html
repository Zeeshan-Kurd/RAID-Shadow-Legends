Group Reflection

It was challenging attempting to assign roles and work for assignment 3 in the beginning as many of us were unsure as to what or where we could use our skills to develop the project and report. This led to our team, wasting away the first few weeks of the assignment as some of our team members were lost on where their place was in the team. This was however, rectified after a discussion with Anthony which led to more open communication in the group and back and forth rapport.


Despite listing it as something our group needed to improve on from Assignment 2, our time management skills are still not significantly better than last time, with us having to “rush” to complete the work during the last few remaining meetings and days before submission, although comparatively to Assignment 2 we did start a little earlier.


It had also proved to be somewhat difficult in procuring a time when all 6 members of the group were able to be present for a productive group meeting, often there would be a single member who had mistaken the time of the meeting or had something come up, leaving only 4 or 5 team members in a call. This led to a lot of rescheduling and a few wasted night where work could have been completed instead of waiting 30 to 45 minutes for a reply from team members who had to inevitably cancel the meeting regardless. If possible, the group would have liked to have a few more recorded Microsoft Team meetings.


Having a new team member who actively participated and offered their insights and ideas throughout the report was also very beneficial as a whole as it allowed new perspectives and skills to be added to the project, mainly knowledge in project management and the construction of time frames, which no other group member was able to produce at the time.


The audit trail on the Git repository is not representative of the group’s efforts as a whole, as the main purpose of the repository was to contain the code that ran the website, which was spearheaded by Zeeshan. The other group members worked on the Word document, writing up the sections that were allocated during our team meetings, and on the AWS server that hosts the Discord bot. Therefore, the Git audit trail on GitHub is not representative of the group’s work on the assignment as the bulk of the work was completed in the shared Word document rather than on GitHub.
