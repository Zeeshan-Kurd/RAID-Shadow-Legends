Group Processes

Overall, from assignment 2 I believe our work rate as a group was like an upstart hill. We had a few issues sorting the group chemistry. Such as finding times we all can have calls, attending classes so that we could have a face-to-face discussion and finishing work at the allocated times. However, as weeks went by our group chemistry improve due to the realization of the amount of work that must be completed and the standard it must be done. We started to pick times where we were all available to get some work done and we were able to allocate work to be done during our own spare time. One major thing that would have helped our efficiency to work, would have been if everyone would have attended the weekly practical lessons, so that we all as a team could have discussed the work that should be done. What was surprising was our ability get the work done in the limited time we had as a group and our willingness to allocate parts of the project to one another. One thing I learnt about this group was how disorganized we were as a team. Changes we have decided upon as a group would be making the effort to attend the practical classes every week and letting the team know beforehand if something happens and unable to attend class. This will allow to think ahead of time to allocate work should be completed so that the person who was unable to attend can finish their part. We also plan to have more consistent teams meeting so that everyone is updated on the work that has be finished. This is due our pervious assignment meeting being infrequent, and members of our group being were unaware of the work that should have been done at the time they had been issued. We have additionally figured out the not all members have mean of communication other than teams, so with have discussed other ways of contacting them for example email, discord, Instagram. This is to ensure that everyone is up to date.

With the addition of a new member, we were glad to find out that they are much more present in class and responsive than our last member of our team who literally disappeared during assignment 2. Paul also brings with him a different perspective and greater set of skills having already completed a diploma of IT, specifically in project management which will be very beneficial in assignment 3.
