Group ReflectionReflection

Dylan

Working in team RAID Shadow Legends has proven to be a challenging journey to find a method that works best for all of us. Initially we had adopted a very relaxed attitude when starting out having thought that this assignment was simply the same as assignment one but collated as a group. There was poor organisation right off the bat, with not many meetings scheduled and no real plan put in place until a few weeks before the due date. This led to a very sudden awakening where all members then adopted a much more alert and switched-on mentality, pushing out a huge amount of work in very short amounts of time. I am very impressed and surprised at how well we worked under the pressure of both an impending deadline as well as a group member being absent for most of the time. It was very pleasing to see and learn that each member of my team was extremely capable of producing a piece of work at a consistent standard when it came down to it all. It is certain that strong, consistent, communication and having a clear plan when starting out is essential to the success of a group and will be noted for the next assignment.

Group Reflection

Unfortunately, our group had a member of our team recurringly not show up to meetings and would be frequently hard to contact. Out of the six online meetings and five in-class sessions we had to work on assignment two, Milan had only attended one in-class session at the beginning and one online meeting (meeting two), after which, he had proceeded to not add anything to the report despite specific allocation and deadlines. It proved to be very difficult attempting to work around a group member who we believed were to be present or had hoped to return and produce some level of work.


Another vice that occurred was disorganisation due to lack of present members in class and online, and due to an underestimation of the volume of work required. This had placed a great amount of stress on the group when we realised how much time we had left to complete a large volume of work. But conversely, this sense of pressure also pushed us to schedule more meetings and put in more hours to complete the work so that we could submit a final product on time.


Despite this initial disorganisation and an absent member, we had discovered that the remainder of the team are able to work extremely well under a tight deadline and significant pressure. Everyone was able to perform their allocated tasks to a determined deadline with minimal tardiness and produce work of a consistent level.


Upon reflecting our progression throughout this assignment, it is clear to all of us that there are many areas of teamwork that we can improve on for assignment 3, mainly organisation and ensuring an early start so that we have more time and headroom to work around any problems which may occur.


The audit trail on the Git repository reflects how our team had decided to divide the work into our key strengths. Allocating the coding of the website to Nikila and Zeeshan who had better designed websites in their A1’s out of all the group members and allocating the remaining group members to work on formatting the report into a more cohesive and visually appealing document. Therefore, the Git audit trail on GitHub is not representative of the group’s work on the assignment as the bulk of the work was completed in the shared Word document rather than on GitHub.
